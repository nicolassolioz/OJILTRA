Original Journalistic Information Label and Traceability

An approach to label content and enable information traceability in order to promote journalistic content and fight against Fake News.

A labled newspaper article will attract more readers

This project aims to develop and evaluate a comprehensive labelling system to identify and certify content originally produced by journalists, in order to promote it and differentiate it from non-journalistic content, especially misinformation. Furthermore, to encourage a broad adoption by the media, the project considers the perception and impact that such a label would have on the readers, and seeks an architecture that leverages existing solutions and integrates seamlessly with the publishing workflows of partner media outlets.

Objectives

We aim to answer the following research questions:

1. Does the establishment of a label promote the quality of digital journalistic online text-based content, and/or increase the trust among the readership?
2. What are the most optimal methods and systems for certifying and authenticating the originality of journalistic content?
3. What is the most optimal approach for deploying an inter-media certification system at regional, national and international level?
